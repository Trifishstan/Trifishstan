The Handle goes through both the small holes on the Sleeve and the small hole on the Gear. Other than that just follow the assembly lines for the rest of the assembly. If there is any confusion on the assembly, please refer to the finished assembly below for more clarity.

Finished Assembly

This is what the Arbor Press should look like once it is fully assembled.

The Table should rotate around the Table Pin with little to no friction, and the Table and Table pin should be easily removeable if desired. The Handle should directly impact the movement of the Rack Pad through the Gear Rack when the Gear is rotated. The Gear gets rotated when the Handle is pushed downward or upward. Turning rotational force into vertical force.

Purpose

An arbor press is a small hand-operated press. It is typically used to perform smaller jobs, such as staking, riveting, installing, configuring and removing bearings and other press fit work. Punches, inserters, or other tools/dies may be added to the end of the ram depending on the desired task.
